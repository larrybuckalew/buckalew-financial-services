5 Retirement Planning Mistakes to Avoid

Published: January 15, 2024 By Larry Buckalew

1. Starting Too Late

One of the most critical mistakes is delaying retirement savings. The power of compound interest means starting early can dramatically increase your retirement funds.

2. Underestimating Expenses

Many people fail to account for healthcare costs, inflation, and lifestyle maintenance in their retirement planning.

3. Ignoring Diversification

Putting all your investments in one type of asset can expose you to unnecessary risk. A diversified portfolio is key to long-term financial stability.

4. Overlooking Tax Implications

Understanding the tax consequences of different retirement accounts can help you optimize your savings strategy.

5. Not Adjusting Your Strategy

Your retirement plan should evolve with your life stages. Regular reviews and adjustments are crucial.
