Medicare Explained

Medicare is a federal health insurance program designed for individuals who are 65 or older, certain younger people with disabilities, and people with End-Stage Renal Disease. Understanding your options is crucial for making informed decisions about your healthcare coverage.

Medicare Part A (Hospital Insurance)

  • Inpatient hospital stays
  • Skilled nursing facility care
  • Hospice care
  • Some home health care

Medicare Part B (Medical Insurance)

  • Doctor visits and outpatient care
  • Preventive services
  • Medical supplies
  • Mental health services

Medicare Part C (Medicare Advantage)

  • Combines Part A and B coverage
  • Often includes Part D drug coverage
  • May offer additional benefits
  • Network-based care options

Medicare Part D (Prescription Drug Coverage)

  • Prescription medication coverage
  • Can be standalone or part of Medicare Advantage
  • Various plan options available
  • Annual enrollment periods

Medicare Supplement Insurance (Medigap)

Medicare Supplement Insurance helps fill the "gaps" in Original Medicare coverage. These policies can help pay for:

  • Deductibles and copayments
  • Coinsurance costs
  • Emergency care when traveling abroad
  • Additional out-of-pocket expenses

Why Consider a Medicare Supplement Plan?

Medicare Supplement plans provide predictable costs and the freedom to see any doctor who accepts Medicare. We can help you compare different plans and find the one that best fits your needs and budget.

Important Medicare Enrollment Periods

Initial Enrollment Period

Starts 3 months before your 65th birthday month and ends 3 months after

Annual Election Period

October 15 - December 7 each year

Open Enrollment Period

January 1 - March 31 each year for Medicare Advantage plans

Special Enrollment Periods

Available in specific situations like moving or losing other coverage
